# Literature Survey

## Paper 1: Temporal Text Classification with Large Language Models (2026)
* **Goal:** Evaluate LLMs on automatic dating of texts (Temporal Text Classification).
* **Dataset:** CLMET (Corpus of Late Modern English Texts), sourced from Project Gutenberg.
* **Timeline:** 1710–1920 (Divided into three 70-year periods).
* **Key Benchmark:** The SVM baseline using TF-IDF vectors achieved a Macro F1-score of **0.78** on 100-token inputs.



## Paper 2: Stylochronometry: Timeline Prediction in Stylometric Analysis
* **Goal:** Measuring change in linguistic style over time within an author's canon.
* **Key Features for Phase 4:** 1. Type-Token Ratio (TTR) - Vocabulary richness.
    2. Average Sentence Length - Complexity of structure.
    3. Word Length - Formal vs. simple vocabulary.
    4. Punctuation Density - Use of semicolons and commas.
* **Result:** Achieved an error margin of ±7.2 years, showing style is a strong temporal marker.
